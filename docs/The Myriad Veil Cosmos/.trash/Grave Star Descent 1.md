# ☄️ Grave Star Descent (坠星葬诀 • Zhuì Xīng Zàng Jué)

> *"From the dead firmament, a star falls not to give light—but to bury all beneath its funeral flare."*

---

## 🌌 Overview

**Grave Star Descent** is a **Desolate Technique** of apocalyptic scale, said to summon or embody the will of a dead celestial body. This technique is rarely recorded and never taught. It is whispered to be **written in the star-scars of the Oblivion Maw**, etched into the bones of fallen True Immortals.

This technique is considered one of the highest expressions of [[Desolate Essence]] in motion—**a burial rite masquerading as an attack**.

## 💀 Description

Upon activation, the cultivator channels the entropy of a celestial corpse—whether literal or metaphysical. A **blackened star** forms in the upper void, pulsing with the weight of forgotten gravities and ruined laws.

### Effects:
- **Cosmic Pressure**: Crushes souls, artifacts, and spirit threads before impact  
- **Temporal Erosion**: Time frays within the target zone; spells collapse, destinies unravel  
- **Desolate Impact**: Upon descent, the star obliterates **form**, **essence**, and **Dao memory**  
- **Aftershock Domain**: Leaves behind a *Grave Halo*—a zone where nothing can cultivate for 10,000 years

---

## 🔬 Components

1. **Grave Intent (葬意 - Zàng Yì)**  
   The user's soul must hold genuine *funereal will*—the desire to entomb all things, not merely destroy.

2. **Star Core Manifestation**  
   A condensed construct of Desolate Essence and Void Qi, forming the **Grave Star** in the sky.

3. **Descent Invocation**  
   Requires the sacrifice of a karmic thread or Dao vow. Without this, the star will shatter the user instead.

4. **Oblivion Binding Seal**  
   Stabilizes the backlash using sigils made from the user's own spirit marrow.

## 🪐 Lore & Myth

> "In the first Grave Star Descent, three heavens died screaming. The cultivator was never found, but the crater still howls."

Some myths claim that Grave Star Descent is **not cast by the cultivator**, but **remembered**—as if reenacting an echo of a stellar death etched into their soul.

Others argue the technique is a **Dao weaponized**, turning nihilism into sacrament.
