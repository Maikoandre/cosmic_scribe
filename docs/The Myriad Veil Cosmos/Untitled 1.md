The winds of the Frigid Immortal School were never kind, carrying with them the biting chill of the surrounding glacial peaks, but today, the atmosphere in the Martial Dao Arena was colder than the ice itself.

Feng Huang stood at the center of the spirit-stone platform, his azure robes fluttering violently around him. His aura was a blazing beacon of spiritual pressure—the Peak of the Golden Core realm. To the thousands of outer and inner disciples watching from the tiered stands, he was a prodigy, a star rising with meteoric brilliance.

But in Feng Huang’s heart, there was only a hungry fire. *Peak Golden Core is not enough,* he thought, his eyes narrowing as he stared across the arena. *To reach the apex, to become the Black Star, I must trample the mountains standing in my way.*

His gaze locked onto his opponent.

Mo Zhengdong stood ten paces away, leaning casually against a heavy, dark-iron spear. He wore the simple grey robes of a standard inner disciple, unadorned and unassuming. Ranked tenth on the Earth Roll, Mo Zhengdong was an enigma—a man who rarely fought, rarely spoke, and seemed to lack the terrifying killing intent of the other top rankers.

"Mo Zhengdong," Feng Huang called out, his voice amplified by qi, echoing like a war drum. "They say you guard the gate of the Top Ten. They say you are steady. Today, I will prove that steadiness is nothing before absolute ambition."

Mo Zhengdong lifted his head. His face was plain, his expression placid as a windless lake. He looked at Feng Huang not with anger, but with a weary patience.

"Junior Brother Feng," Mo Zhengdong said, his voice soft yet carrying clearly through the howling wind. "Cultivation is a long road. There is no need to rush toward a cliff."

"Arrogance!" Feng Huang scoffed. "You speak of cliffs because you lack the wings to fly!"

*Boom!*

Feng Huang stomped his foot. The spiritual energy in his dantian erupted. A phantom image of a Frost Phoenix screeched behind him, dropping the temperature of the arena so low that frost crept over the protective barriers.

"Frozen Heaven Arts: Glacial Strike!"

Feng Huang vanished, replaced by a streak of icy blue light. He was fast—faster than any Golden Core cultivator had the right to be. His sword, wrapped in the chill of the netherworld, slashed toward Mo Zhengdong’s throat. It was a killing blow, one intended to end the fight instantly and secure his fame.

 The crowd gasped. *Too fast!*

But Mo Zhengdong did not retreat. He did not form a hand seal. He did not summon a defensive artifact.

He simply exhaled.

*Crack.*

The sound was not loud, yet it resonated in the chests of everyone present, like the popping of a heavy joint. Mo Zhengdong’s skin suddenly shimmered with a dull, bronze luster. Arcs of purple lightning, thick as snakes, surged from his pores, wrapping around his body and his spear.

He didn't swing the spear. He merely shifted his grip and slammed the butt of the shaft into the stone floor.

*Thrum!*

An invisible shockwave of pure physical force and lightning qi exploded outward.

Feng Huang, mid-lunging, felt as though he had slammed face-first into an invisible mountain range. The Frost Phoenix phantom shattered into motes of light. His sword qi disintegrated. Before his blade could even graze Mo Zhengdong’s robe, the sheer pressure of the counter-force blasted him backward.

"Urgh!"

Feng Huang tumbled through the air, crashing into the barrier at the edge of the ring. He slid down, coughing up a mouthful of blood, his internal organs trembling from the impact.

Silence descended upon the arena. Absolute, terrified silence.

One move. It wasn't even an attack; it was a release of presence.

Mo Zhengdong stood exactly where he had been, the lightning fading beneath his skin, the spear resting dormant once more. He looked at the gasping Feng Huang with eyes that held the depth of the abyss.

Slowly, the Tenth Disciple walked forward. He stopped a few paces from Feng Huang and looked up at the sky, where the clouds churned darkly.

"You speak of the Black Star," Mo Zhengdong said, his tone devoid of mockery, carrying only a solemn weight. "You wish to challenge the Heavens with the body of a mortal."

Mo Zhengdong tapped the iron shaft of his spear, a rhythmic beat that matched the pounding of Feng Huang’s heart. Then, he recited, his voice echoing like distant thunder:

*"The frog in the well sees a circle of sky,*
*And mocks the eagle that soars up high.*
*You strive to shake the mountain’s base,*
*With arms of straw and a frantic pace.*

*The abyss below, the heavens above,*
*Are not conquered by pride, nor pushed with a shove.*
*If my humble light can blind your eyes,*
*How will you bear the sun when it dares to rise?"*

Feng Huang grit his teeth, humiliation burning hotter than his injuries. "You... you mock me?"

"I warn you," Mo Zhengdong replied, turning to leave. "I am merely the tenth. A gatekeeper. The one who sits upon the throne of the Black Star... the gap between him and me is as vast as the gap between me and you. Do not seek to shoulder the weight of the sky before your bones have turned to iron."

Feng Huang trembled, clutching his chest. He wanted to retort, to scream that he would surpass them all, but suddenly, a familiar, ancient voice echoed directly inside his soul—resonating from the spatial ring on his finger.

*“He is right, little brat,”* the mysterious master’s voice rasped, heavy with disappointment.

Feng Huang froze. *Master?*

*“You were a fool,”* the Master continued, his spiritual voice sharp as a blade. *“You saw a ranking number and assumed weakness. You did not see that his blood qi flows like a dormant river of mercury. That boy... he is a Body Cultivator on the verge of the Soul Formation realm, suppressing his cultivation to temper his foundation. He defeated you with less than one-tenth of his strength.”*

Feng Huang’s pupils dilated. *Suppressed? One-tenth?*

*“Heed his poem,”* the Master chided. *“'The abyss below, the heavens above.' You are currently an ant trying to trip an elephant. If this Mo Zhengdong is merely the tenth, hiding such power... then this generation of the Frigid Immortal School is not a gathering of geniuses.”*

The Master’s voice lowered to a grave whisper.

*“It is a gathering of monsters.”*

Feng Huang watched Mo Zhengdong’s retreating back, the simple grey figure disappearing into the mist. The pain in his body was nothing compared to the sudden, freezing realization in his heart. The world he thought he understood was a lie. The peak he aimed for was not a mountain top; it was a star suspended in a void so vast it threatened to swallow him whole.

For the first time since embarking on the Dao, Feng Huang felt true fear.
